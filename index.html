Machine Learning:
A type of artificial intelligence that allows computer systems to learn and improve from experience without being explicitly programmed. Machine learning algorithms can analyze data, identify patterns, and make predictions or decisions based on that data.
Virtual Reality:
A computer-generated simulation of an environment that can be experienced through a headset or other devices. Virtual reality technology can create immersive, interactive experiences that simulate real-world environments or imaginary ones.
Cloud Computing:
A model of delivering computing services over the internet, rather than through on-premises hardware or software. Cloud computing allows users to access computing resources such as servers, storage, and applications on demand, and pay only for what they use.
Blockchain:
A distributed ledger technology that allows multiple parties to record and verify transactions without the need for a central authority. Blockchain technology is most commonly associated with cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin, but has potential applications in many other industries.
Internet of Things (IoT)
A network of interconnected physical devices, vehicles, buildings, and other objects that are embedded with sensors, software, and connectivity, allowing them to collect and exchange data. IoT technology has the potential to transform many industries, from healthcare to manufacturing to transportation.
